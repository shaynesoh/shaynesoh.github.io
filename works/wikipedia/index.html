Redesigning The Talk Page

Project type:
Design Challenge

Role:
User research, UX/UI Design, Prototyping

Duration:
1 week

The Problem

Wikipedia is an open online encyclopedia that is collaboratively maintained by a community of volunteer editors and is funded primarily through donations. Despite its success and popularity, Wikipedia is prone to inconsistent quality and accuracy in some domains. Additionally, Wikipedia also suffers from systemic bias given the lack of representation from certain demographic concentrations.

One reason for this is the lack of accessibility for less technically adept groups. Until recently, users are required to use wiki markup for editing on Wikipedia, which can be challenging for thoses with no prior programming knowledge. And while Wikipedia has since introduced an alternative VisualEditor which does not require the use of wiki markup, the overall editing experience on the Wikipedia talk page remains difficult to navigate for potential contributors.

The Challenge

Each article on Wikipedia contains a dedicated talk page where editors can discuss changes and or edits regarding the corresponding article with one another.

However, it can be tricky to navigate, particularly when there are many different ongoing discussions, and starting a new discussion can be intimidating for new users.

How might the Wikipedia talk page be redesigned to make it easier for editors to participate?

What Is A Talk Page?

Talk pages are pages where editors can discuss improvements to articles or other Wikipedia pages. Each article is accompanied by a talk page that is named by adding "Talk:" before the article title (eg. Talk:Wikipedia).

There are two ways to access a talk page:

  1. By accessing the "Talk" tab at the top of the article page or,
  2. By adding "Talk:" before the article title in the URL.

Heuristic Evaluation

The current talk page environment is overly complicated to use and grasp, especially for new users

As someone who's new to the talk page, I wanted to familiarise myself with the editing process. So after trying it out, I mapped out the key steps of the editing process in the table below.

I then identified the major usability issues in the current interface, basing my observations around Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics for user-interface design.

Current Editing Process Current Steps
Starting a new thread
  • Click "New section" link at the top.
  • Enter header and content.
  • Add "~~~~" at the end of each comment or click the signature button.
  • Click "Publish changes".
Edit or reply to existing threads
  • Click the "Edit" link.
  • Add comments below the last entry or below a specific response.
  • Add "~~~~" at the end of each comment or click the signature button.
  • Click "Publish changes".
Notifying other users
  • Type "{{Reply to|Username}}".
  • Add "~~~~" at the end of each comment or click the signature button.
  • Click "Publish changes".

Pain point #1
Visual and content clutter
Overall, the talk page is difficult to digest given the disorganised layout and overly cluttered content. Also, the new section link is visually inconspicuous and can be easily missed.

Pain point #2
Need to recall instead of recognition
Users sometimes forget to sign off (add ~~~~ at the end of comment), which makes it difficult to tell at times who's replying to who and identify comments pertaining to an individual.

Pain point #3
Poor learnability for new users
The editing comments and conventions can be complex and difficult to remember since they are only applicable on Wikipedia. Also, the more user-friendly VisualEditor only applies to articles and not Talk Pages.

Pain point #4
Unintuitive editing process
Users cannot reply directly to a comment; they can only do so by making edits to the entire thread as a whole on a new page which also complicates the information back into code.

User Interviews

All of our interviewees struggled to understand and navigate around the current Talk Page

Given the limited time available, I conducted usability testing of the current app with 5 interviewees who are new to the Wikipedia Talk Page in order to validate what the pain points I'd previously highlighted. I also wanted to uncover other pain points that real users faced when using the Talk Page and explore the users' needs.

  • Pain point #1
    Lack of convenient reply feature

    "Kind of annoying that I have to edit the entire thread on a new page just to reply to a comment."

  • Pain point #2
    Wiki Markup is difficult to comprehend

    "Can you explain what these symbols mean? I don't get it."

  • Pain point #3
    Lack of help documentation intimidates new users and leads to poorly formatted edits

    "Is there like a tutorial I can refer to?"

  • Pain point #4
    Difficult to navigate long threads which also complicates editing in Wikitext markup

    "Oh my god, I can't tell what's going on anymore, I give up."

User Personas

Based on the research insights, I came up with 2 user personas that best represents the demographics that are most likely to be affected by the redesign, along with their needs and frustrations with the current user interface.

  • Jimmy, 22
    Junior Contributor

    As a copywriter, Jimmy really enjoy writing articles as a hobby. Recently, he became a fan of a new Sci-fi TV show and wants to improve information on this particular topic of interest on Wikipedia. However, Jimmy struggles to figure out how to navigate the Talk Page, let alone start a discussion.

    "Why is it so difficult to contribute to the Talk Pages on Wikipedia? The learning curve is steep and I can't seem to figure it out at all."

    Needs:

    • Find an easier way to contribute and communicate on Talk pages.
    • Minimise typos, inaccuracies and other mistakes.

    Frustrations:

    • Struggle to memorise the talk page editing conventions and comments.
    • Find the page difficult to navigate, understand, and use.
    • Frequently make editing errors.

  • Scott, 41
    Senior Contributor

    Scott has been editing Wikipedia since he was in college. As a long time contributor to the Wikipedia community, he frequently participate in community discussions and help out new contributors. Despite the launch of the new VisualEditor, Scott notices that new contributors still continue to have a hard time getting used to the Talk Pages.

    “It's kind of annoying if I have to frequently amend the errors made by new members and direct them to the appropiate help documentation."

    Needs:

    • For junior contributors to acquaint themselves with the editing environment without much assistance
    • To ensure that discussions on Talk Pages remain productive and go on smoothly.

    Frustrations:

    • Frequently amend editing errors and provide help to junior contributors.
    • Finds it difficult to reply in lengthy conversations as many users forget to sign off their posts.

Which Flows To Redesign?

To tackle my problem statement, contributors (across varying skill levels) need to intuitively understand the site and know how to participate in and continue conversations.

My goal will therefore be to enhance the existing communication tools to make it easier for all contributors to work together

This can be achieved by:

  • Familiarising with the editing tools,
  • Properly typing and formatting posts according to the community guidelines and,
  • Correctly replying to specific posts

What's The Design Approach?

The redesign should not dismiss Wikipedia’s core functionality as an online encyclopedia. It should continue to work inclusively for everyone, feel familiar for the long-time users/readers, and be easy-to-use and understand for new users.

To achieve my intended goals, I gave myself the following goals and set myself some appropiate design constraints that are as follows:

  • Design goal #1:
    Minimise additional bandwidth for editors with data constraints

    Design constraint:
    Minimize JavaScript, CSS styling, and other scripts

  • Design goal #2:
    Make editing easy for new users without sidelining existing contributors

    Design constraint:
    Build upon the existing functionality instead of a total revamp

  • Design goal #3:
    Avoid changing the current structure and information density entirely

    Design constraint:
    Reuse and or add-onto existing HTML structure

Working On Ideas

Having established a general direction, I sketched some ideas based on the research findings. My intention was to make the talk page easier to read and edit by:

  • Adding visual indicators to distinguish information between users,
  • Enabling users to directly reply to a specific post instead of editing the entire thread,
  • Simplifying complex commands into dropdown options and,
  • Allowing users to switch between VisualEditor and wikitext editor.

Reflections

Takeaways

At times, I did find myself fussing over the lack of fancy UI elements and interface for this particular project. However, a lot of good UX is a result of great listening and most problems come from overthinking and prettifying things without a clear reason to do so. Good UX is about simplicity and acommodating your users' needs more than anything else.
